BCI-HRP // The Human Return Protocol

Wei Jueran (魏珏然) · Mellow — M.S.Ed. in Learning Sciences and Technologies, University of Pennsylvania GSE

A protocol-first, lab-facing research proposal testing whether sound probes, self-prediction, and editable AI naming can improve within-person self-calibration — with autonomic and neural signals as optional proxies, not as hidden truth.

Current scope: Phase −1 Pause-QDR baseline + Phase 0 Sound-QDR software protocol. Custom BCI hardware is future-facing, not required for the pilot.

No diagnosis No treatment No state induction Not a consumer device Protocol-first Independent proposal
Read the 3-min brief

One testable claim

A single falsifiable question, before anything else.

Does a sound-probe + self-prediction + editable-naming protocol improve within-person self-calibration — and do autonomic/neural proxies add explanatory value beyond self-report alone?

Research stance. The first study is feasibility and construct calibration, not clinical validation. “Self-calibration” and “Human Return” are candidate constructs — a partner lab is free to narrow, rename, reject, or revise them before preregistration.

Naming boundary

BCI-HRP is used here as the project name and long-range research direction. The immediate pilot is not a custom BCI build. The first empirical object is the protocol: prediction → probe → report → editable naming → agency events.

Not a device pitch

The first study does not require any custom hardware.

Not mind-reading

Proxies do not reveal meaning or “true” emotion.

Not fixed doctrine

The lab has authority to redesign the construct.

Not therapy

No treatment or crisis-detection claim is made.

Why this matters

Most systems either over-interpret the user or under-describe experience.

Emotion tools often force people into coarse labels like calm or stressed. Consumer neurotech often overclaims hidden insight from weak physiological proxies. Music tools often optimize for mood playlists without modeling a person's prediction, interpretive control, or safe exit. This protocol aims at the missing middle: sound becomes a controlled probe, the user predicts first, physiology stays a proxy, and AI explanations stay editable and rejectable.

Gap 01

Too much reliance on coarse affect labels; too little on qualia granularity.

Gap 02

Too much sensor authority; too little user-owned interpretation.

Gap 03

Too much sound-as-mood-control; too little sound-as-probe with falsifiable outcomes.

Gap 04

Too much ethics rhetoric; too little interaction-level safety design.

Scientific layer model

Keep the architecture rich, but scientifically legible.

One sound-response session moves through seven layers, from the physical stimulus to user-owned interpretation.

01

Sound event

The actual stimulus: a fixed audio file with documented acoustic properties and event markers.

02

Sensory contact

How the sound enters — hearing, bodily resonance, respiration coupling, attentional capture.

03

Autonomic / body response

HRV, EDA, movement, temperature, and body self-report as within-person response traces.

04

Affective construction

How bodily change becomes felt pressure, relief, activation, safety, or overwhelm.

05

Cognitive prediction

What the user expected before the sound, and whether prediction error decreases over sessions.

06

Meaning making

Memory, future direction, autobiographical resonance, or value-significance notes.

07

Interpretive agency

Accept, edit, reject, rename, delete, stop, ground, and skip — as user-owned interpretive control.

Protocol snapshot

A PI should be able to judge fit in three minutes.

Core unit

Session, not device

The research unit is one sound-response session: prediction, sound probe, report, editable naming, and agency events.

Primary test

Prediction error

Whether within-person pre-probe prediction error changes across sessions and transfers beyond memorized clips.

Safety principle

Agency before interpretation

AI summaries are always editable and rejectable. Stop, Ground, Delete, Rename, and Skip are primary data — not failures.

Confound control — stimulus familiarity vs. self-calibration

Because the sound files are fixed and repeated, a fall in prediction error could reflect memorizing a specific clip rather than improved self-calibration. To separate the two: (1) test prediction on unheard stimuli from the same family (a transfer test); (2) and/or analyze first-exposure prediction error only; (3) and include a novel-vs-familiar factor in the model. State-naming stability carries the same familiarity confound and is reported with the same control.

T5

User accepts, edits, rejects, renames, deletes, or exits.

Phase 2

Optional EEG / fNIRS

Operational definitions

Make “Human Return” measurable without reducing it to a hidden signal.

Primary

Self-prediction error

Pre-probe predicted vector vs. post-probe reported vector. Candidate dimensions: tension, clarity, body return, movement urge, emotional pressure, safety. Direction match + normalized absolute error.

Secondary

State-naming stability

Across repeated sound families, whether the user reuses, refines, or stabilizes personal labels without being pulled into AI labels.

Construct

Qualia granularity

A shift from generic terms (“good / bad”) toward differentiated bodily–affective–perceptual–action descriptions.

Agency

Interpretive agency

Stop, Ground, Delete, Reject, Rename, Skip, and Edit recorded as primary events, not UI afterthoughts.

What stays optional

EEG/fNIRS, custom head-patch form factors, and full triadic hardware should remain optional until a protocol-first pilot shows enough value to justify the engineering complexity.

What can already be done

A fixed sound library, pre/post self-prediction scales, qualitative naming prompts, agency-event logging, and an exportable session schema are already enough to start Phase 0.

Safety interaction flow

Ethics must appear as interaction, not only as principle.

1

Consent gate

The user chooses sound family, intensity ceiling, stop method, data sharing, and whether AI summaries are allowed.

2

Persistent controls

Stop, Ground, Volume Down, Skip, and Delete stay visible before, during, and after playback.

3

Auto-pause triggers

Unsafe self-report, repeated stop gestures, non-response, rapid discomfort increase, or a lab-defined physiology threshold.

4

Grounding mode

Fade out sound, suppress interpretation, show a grounding prompt, offer a researcher check-in.

5

No crisis system

This is not emergency detection or therapy. High-risk disclosures leave the protocol and follow lab-approved human safety procedures.

UI requirement

Always-visible exit

Stop is larger than decorative controls, keyboard/touch accessible, and logged as a valid outcome — not a failure.

UI requirement

No post-trigger analysis

If grounding mode triggers, the system produces no deep interpretation — only a summary of the user's own safety actions.

UI requirement

Agency as data

Reject, rename, stop, delete, and skip are core interaction events, not afterthoughts.

Preliminary Pause-QDR dataset

Before sound probes, pause itself must be measured.

This exploratory N≈10 static-pause dataset is not evidence that the protocol is effective. It is Phase −1 feasibility material: it shows that participants can complete pre/post self-report, produce rich qualia notes, and reveal confounds such as task demand, environment, phone absence, and pause-only effects.

Data file clarification

data_schema.csv is not the static-pause dataset. It is a future protocol data dictionary / field template. The actual preliminary pause-only summary is pause_qdr_preliminary_summary.csv, with interpretation in pause_qdr_preliminary_dataset.md.

Dataset type

Pause-only baseline

Roughly 10–12 minutes of pausing ordinary tasks, with pre/post mood, focus, and body-state ratings plus open notes.

Preliminary signal

Body relaxation is strongest

Descriptively, mean body-relaxation gain is 2.11 across N=9; mood and focus shifts are heterogeneous and should not be overclaimed.

Design implication

Add a pause-only baseline

Future Sound-QDR pilots should separate pause, context, task-demand, acoustic, and interpretive-agency effects.

Correct use

Use this dataset to justify baseline design and qualitative coding — not to claim meditation efficacy, clinical benefit, sound-probe efficacy, or physiological validation. Raw notes are kept private; only anonymized derived summaries are linked below.

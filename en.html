BCI - The Human Return Protocol

A lab-facing protocol for testing whether sound probes, self-prediction, editable naming, and wearable/neural proxies can improve within-person self-calibration.

Current pilot: Sound-QDR first. Existing lab equipment second. Future triadic hardware last. This is not a consumer BCI product, not diagnosis, not treatment, and not state induction.

One Testable Claim

Does a sound-probe + self-prediction + editable naming protocol improve within-person self-calibration, and do autonomic/neural proxies add explanatory value beyond self-report alone?

Research stance: this is feasibility and construct calibration, not clinical validation. “Qualia alignment” and “Human Return” are candidate constructs that can be narrowed, renamed, rejected, or revised by a partner lab before preregistration.

Why This Matters

Most systems either over-interpret the user or under-describe experience.

Emotion tools often force users into coarse labels like calm / stressed. Consumer neurotech often overclaims hidden insight from weak physiological proxies. Music tools often optimize for mood playlists without modeling user prediction, interpretive control, or safe exit. BCI-HRP Lab targets the missing middle: sound as a controlled probe, user prediction first, physiology as proxy, and AI explanations as editable and rejectable.

Research Gap
Gap 01

Too much reliance on coarse affect labels; too little on qualia granularity.

Gap 02

Too much sensor authority; too little user-owned interpretation.

Gap 03

Too much sound-as-mood-control; too little sound-as-probe-with-falsifiable outcomes.

Gap 04

Too much ethics rhetoric; too little interaction-level safety design.

Scientific Layer Model

Keep the architecture rich, but scientifically legible.

Layer 01 — Sound Event

A fixed audio file with documented acoustic properties and event markers.

Layer 02 — Sensory Contact

How sound enters through hearing, bodily resonance, respiration coupling, and attention.

Layer 03 — Autonomic / Body Response

HRV, EDA, movement, temperature, and body self-report as within-person response traces.

Layer 04 — Affective Construction

How bodily change becomes felt pressure, relief, activation, safety, or overwhelm.

Layer 05 — Cognitive Prediction

What the user thought would happen before the sound, and whether prediction error decreases.

Layer 06 — Meaning Making

Memory, future direction, autobiographical resonance, or value-significance notes.

Layer 07 — Interpretive Agency

Accept, edit, reject, rename, delete, stop, ground, and skip.

Protocol Snapshot

A PI should be able to decide whether this is worth a fit call in three minutes.

Core unit

Session, not device

One sound-response session: prediction, sound probe, report, editable naming, and agency events.

Primary test

Prediction error

Whether within-person pre-probe prediction error changes across sessions and transfers beyond memorized clips.

Safety principle

Agency before interpretation

AI summaries are editable and rejectable. Stop, Ground, Delete, Rename, and Skip are primary data.

Pilot Design

Small, within-subject, ablated.

Study typeWithin-subject feasibility pilot; repeated sessions; non-clinical participants; no treatment claim.

Operational Definitions

Make “Human Return” measurable without reducing it to a hidden signal.

Self-prediction error

Pre-probe predicted vector compared with post-probe reported vector. Candidate dimensions: tension, clarity, body return, movement urge, emotional pressure, safety.

Qualia granularity

Shift from generic terms (“good / bad”) to differentiated bodily-affective-perceptual-action descriptions.

State-naming stability

Whether users reuse, refine, or stabilize personal labels without being forced into AI labels.

Interpretive agency

Stop, Ground, Delete, Reject, Rename, Skip, and Edit are primary data events, not UI afterthoughts.

Preliminary Pause-QDR Dataset

Before sound probes, pause itself must be measured.

This exploratory N≈10 static-pause dataset is not evidence that BCI-HRP or Sound-QDR is effective. It is Phase -1 feasibility material: participants can complete pre/post self-report, produce rich qualia notes, and reveal confounds such as task demand, environment, phone absence, and pause-only effects.

Dataset type

Pause-only baseline

Roughly 10–12 minutes of pausing ordinary tasks with pre/post mood, focus, body-state ratings and open notes.

Preliminary signal

Body relaxation is strongest

Descriptively, mean body-relaxation gain is 2.11 across N=9; mood and focus shifts are heterogeneous and should not be overclaimed.

Design implication

Add pause-only baseline

Future Sound-QDR pilots should separate pause, context, task-demand, acoustic, and interpretive-agency effects.

Safety Interaction Flow

Ethics must appear as interaction, not only as principle.

Consent gate

User chooses sound family, intensity ceiling, stop method, data sharing, and AI-summary permission.

Persistent controls

Stop / Ground / Volume Down / Skip / Delete remain visible.

Auto-pause

Unsafe self-report, repeated stop, non-response, discomfort increase, or lab-defined threshold.

No crisis system

High-risk disclosures leave the protocol and follow lab-approved human safety procedures.

Collaboration Model

Not asking a lab to execute a finished system.

What I bring

  • Human Return / QDR construct and interface logic.
  • Qualia naming and interpretive-agency design.
  • Sound-probe interaction prototype and narrative design.
  • AI hypotheses as editable, rejectable, and deletable.

What a lab brings

  • Methodological rigor, IRB, and safety review.
  • Sensor selection, signal processing, artifact criteria.
  • Recruitment, statistical modeling, preregistration.
  • Authority to narrow, rename, reject, or split hypotheses.
First Ask

A 15-minute fit call.

The first meeting is not a funding ask and not a hardware ask. It is a fit call to decide whether this belongs as a software protocol, wearable feasibility pilot, HCI safety study, or music-cognition / neuroaesthetics project.
